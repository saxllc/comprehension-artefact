India records consent.
It does not record understanding.

Digital public infrastructure built the world's best plumbing for permission. The missing record is comprehension — and India is the only country positioned to standardise it.

By Sridhar Dhulipala · designed and launched the new Aadhaar app at UIDAI (2023–25) · founder, sax.llc
Scroll
A woman in a semi-urban town reaches the consent screen of a lending app.
She reads it, or tries to. She turns to her husband. He skims it. He nods.
She taps “I Agree.” Her Aadhaar details move to a finance company, irreversibly — and somewhere a consent artefact is written: precise, machine-readable, cryptographically auditable.
No record anywhere captures whether she understood what she permitted.

This is not an edge case. At India's scale — twenty-two scheduled languages, a Language Diversity Index of 0.93, six meaningfully different literacy bands — the gap between captured consent and actual comprehension is the median case.

Credit where due

India solved permission.

The consent artefact gave permission a standard form: open, revocable, granular, auditable. The Account Aggregator framework operationalised it. The data protection law made consent managers a regulated category. No other country has plumbing this good for permission.

But the entire stack records a decision while remaining silent on whether a decision, in any cognitive sense, occurred.

Legal scholars reviewing consent managers have already named the risk: a “consent mirage” — opt-ins accumulating without understanding. The law gestures at the problem (notices must be available in scheduled languages), but translation is not comprehension. A perfectly translated paragraph of legalese is still legalese.

Prior art

Other fields solved this decades ago — and wrote down how.

Medicine

The teach-back

Informed-consent law requires consent in a language the patient understands — and clinical practice built the instrument to verify it: the patient explains the procedure back in their own words before signing. Cheap, validated across hundreds of studies, and framed as a test of the explanation, not the patient.

Banking · India

Key Facts Statement

Created because loan disclosures were technically complete and functionally unread. It compresses a loan's terms into a page a borrower can actually parse — regulation moving from disclosure toward comprehension.

Europe · GDPR

Plain language, or invalid

Consent requests must be in “clear and plain language” — unintelligible consent is legally void. The burden of being understood sits with the system, not the citizen.

Europe · AI Act

Transparency duties for AI

AI systems now carry explicit obligations to be understandable to the people they affect. The same wound, twenty-four languages, no instrument.

0% → 0%
When consent forms in Odisha added audio explanations in Odia, reported patient understanding of treatment risk rose from roughly a third to nearly ninety percent. Comprehension is measurable — and language and modality move the number dramatically.
Every one of these instruments mandates the input — plain language, the right language, a summary sheet. None of them records the outcome: did this person, at this moment, understand this action?
The proposal

That outcome can be a record.

Call it the comprehension artefact — the human-readable counterpart to the consent artefact.

Exists today

Consent Artefact

“Permission was granted.”
  • These data items, this purpose
  • This recipient, this expiry
  • Machine-readable, signed, auditable
  • Records the transaction's legality
Proposed

Comprehension Artefact

“Understanding was verified.”
  • Explained in Hindi, grade-6 level, by voice and text
  • User correctly answered: shared data cannot be recalled
  • Two attempts, forty seconds
  • Records the transaction's legitimacy
One without the other is a signature on a page nobody read.
Paired, they make consent mean what it claims to mean.
Design principle one

The unit of adaptation is the moment — not the user.

Persona-based design collapses at a billion users. What scales is classifying the materiality of each step, so friction lands only where the stakes do.

Informational

Browsing a screen. The system stays invisible.

Navigational

Choosing an option. Light guidance, an undo path.

Transactional

Submitting, paying. A plain-language account of what happens next.

Consequential

Irreversible acts — biometric sharing, loan agreements. A verified checkpoint.

Where this goes

Three consequences, one frontier.

Assisted consentDesign principle two

A failed check routes to help, not to a wall — a family member, a banking correspondent, a support line. This honours a reality every Indian designer knows and no Western framework models: comprehension here is often a social act. The person tapping the screen is not always the person deciding. The artefact can record that too — who assisted, in what role — turning the most uncomfortable fact about consent at scale into structured, auditable data.

A sovereign data assetThe exhaust is the alignment layer

Every checkpoint outcome is a labeled data point: this explanation, in this language, at this reading level, did or did not land with this kind of user. Aggregated, that is the largest corpus of verified Indic comprehension data ever assembled — exactly the alignment-layer data Indian language models lack, generated as a by-product of protecting users.

CognitiveVaRIn development

Finance prices market risk with Value-at-Risk. Comprehension failure is also a quantifiable exposure: every mis-sold loan, every “I didn't know it would auto-debit,” every ombudsman case is a comprehension failure surfacing downstream at its most expensive point. Cognitive Value-at-Risk is the expected loss from mis-comprehension across a portfolio of transactions. Today it is real and unpriced. The comprehension artefact makes it measurable — and what becomes measurable becomes manageable, insurable, and ultimately a line a regulator can ask about.

The exportThe UPI playbook

The first wave of DPI exported permission infrastructure; UPI and the consent artefact are studied worldwide. The comprehension artefact is the natural successor — Europe's AI transparency rules, mis-selling settlements everywhere, every consent banner ever clicked unread. Whoever defines the metric defines the standard.

The objection

“Checkpoints add friction.”

The worry

Verification slows the funnel. A recorded failure obligates the platform to act on it. Institutions will resist creating a record that can pause their own conversion.

The answer

The liability already exists — it is simply unpriced. Comprehension failures currently surface downstream as fraud claims, complaints and fines. The artefact moves the cost upstream, where it is smaller, and gives the institution something it has never had: proof that it explained itself.

India defined how a billion people grant permission.
It should now define how systems prove they were understood.

The models are ready — sovereign, multilingual, voice-first. The missing piece is a ten-page specification: a schema, a materiality taxonomy, a verification method, conformance tests. A working proof of concept already exists.
